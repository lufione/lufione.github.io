How Do Youtubers Get Paid With Youtube Premium

Written by Eveline May 22, 2023 Â· 6 min read

YouTube has become one of the most popular platforms for content creators to showcase their talent and reach a global audience. With over 2 billion monthly active users, YouTube has become a lucrative platform for creators to monetize their content and earn a living. One of the ways in which YouTubers get paid is through YouTube Premium. In this article, we will discuss how YouTubers get paid with YouTube Premium.

What is YouTube Premium?

YouTube Premium is a subscription-based service that allows users to watch ad-free videos, access exclusive content, and download videos for offline viewing. It also offers access to YouTube Music, YouTube Originals, and YouTube Kids. The service was launched in 2018 and has since gained popularity among users who want an uninterrupted viewing experience.

How Do YouTubers Get Paid With YouTube Premium?

YouTubers get paid with YouTube Premium in two ways. The first way is through the YouTube Partner Program (YPP), and the second way is through YouTube Premium revenue share. Let's discuss both of these methods in detail.

1. YouTube Partner Program (YPP)

The YouTube Partner Program (YPP) is a program that allows creators to monetize their content through ads. YouTube pays a percentage of the ad revenue to the creator for every view on their videos. To be eligible for the YPP, creators need to have at least 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours in the past 12 months. Once they meet the eligibility criteria, they can apply to join the YPP.

When a user with a YouTube Premium subscription watches a YPP creator's video, the creator gets paid a share of the subscription revenue based on how much time the user spends watching their videos. This means that the more watch time a creator has, the more they will earn from YouTube Premium revenue share.

2. YouTube Premium Revenue Share

The second way YouTubers get paid with YouTube Premium is through YouTube Premium revenue share. YouTube pays a portion of its subscription revenue to creators based on how much time YouTube Premium subscribers spend watching their content. This means that creators with more watch time and engagement will earn more from YouTube Premium revenue share.

The amount of money YouTubers earn from YouTube Premium revenue share varies depending on various factors such as the number of subscribers, engagement on their videos, and the amount of time YouTube Premium subscribers spend watching their content. However, creators can earn anywhere from a few cents to several dollars per view.

Conclusion

YouTubers can earn a significant amount of money through YouTube Premium. However, to earn money from YouTube Premium, creators need to have a large following and create engaging content that attracts viewers. By joining the YouTube Partner Program and creating quality content, creators can monetize their content and earn a living from YouTube Premium revenue share.

In conclusion, YouTube Premium is a great way for creators to monetize their content and earn a living. By creating engaging content and building a loyal following, YouTubers can earn a significant amount of money from YouTube Premium revenue share.
