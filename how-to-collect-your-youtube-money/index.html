How To Collect Your Youtube Money

Written by William Jun 20, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Introduction

If you're a content creator on YouTube, then you know that one of the biggest perks is the potential to earn money from your videos. But once you start earning that money, how do you actually collect it? In this article, we'll walk you through the steps you need to take to make sure you're getting paid for your hard work.

Step 1: Set Up Your AdSense Account

Before you can start collecting money from your YouTube videos, you need to set up an AdSense account. AdSense is the program that YouTube uses to pay creators. You'll need to provide some basic information, like your name and address, and then link your AdSense account to your YouTube channel.

Step 1.1: Meet the Requirements

To qualify for AdSense, you need to have at least 1,000 subscribers and 4,000 watch hours in the past 12 months. If you haven't met these requirements yet, focus on growing your audience and creating engaging content.

Step 1.2: Link Your Accounts

To link your AdSense account to your YouTube channel, go to the "Monetization" tab in your YouTube Studio dashboard. From there, you'll be able to follow the steps to link your accounts.

Step 2: Enable Monetization on Your Videos

Once your AdSense account is set up and linked to your YouTube channel, you need to enable monetization on your videos. This means that ads will be shown on your videos, and you'll earn money based on how many clicks and views those ads get.

Step 2.1: Meet the Guidelines

To enable monetization, your videos need to follow YouTube's community guidelines and advertiser-friendly content guidelines. Make sure your content is appropriate and doesn't violate any of these guidelines.

Step 2.2: Enable Monetization

To enable monetization on your videos, go to the "Monetization" tab in your YouTube Studio dashboard. From there, you'll be able to turn on monetization for each individual video.

Step 3: Track Your Earnings

Once your AdSense account is linked and monetization is enabled, you'll start earning money from your videos. But how do you know how much you're earning?

Step 3.1: Check Your Analytics

YouTube provides a detailed analytics dashboard that shows you how much money you're earning from each video. You can see how many views and clicks each video is getting, as well as how much money you've earned over time.

Step 3.2: Understand Your Revenue Sources

There are a few different ways you can earn money from your YouTube videos. You'll earn money from ads that are shown on your videos, as well as from YouTube Premium subscribers who watch your videos. Make sure you understand where your revenue is coming from so you can optimize your content and earn more money.

Step 4: Get Paid

Finally, once you've earned some money from your YouTube videos, it's time to get paid!

Step 4.1: Set Up Payment Information

To get paid, you'll need to set up your payment information in your AdSense account. You can choose to receive payments via direct deposit, wire transfer, or check.

Step 4.2: Meet the Payment Threshold

Before you can receive your earnings, you need to meet the payment threshold. This is the amount of money you need to earn before you can get paid. The payment threshold varies by country, but in the US, it's $100.

Step 4.3: Receive Your Payment

Once you've met the payment threshold, you'll receive your earnings on the next payment date. Payment dates vary by country, but in the US, payments are made on the 21st of each month.

Conclusion

Collecting your YouTube money may seem like a daunting task, but it's actually a fairly straightforward process. By setting up your AdSense account, enabling monetization, tracking your earnings, and meeting the payment threshold, you'll be able to earn money from your YouTube videos and get paid for your hard work.
