How To Make Money As A Musician In 2023

Written by Eveline Aug 24, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Being a musician can be a rewarding career, but it can also be challenging to make a living from your passion. With the rise of digital platforms and streaming services, there are more opportunities than ever before to monetize your music. In this article, we will explore some of the best ways to make money as a musician in 2023.

Create and Monetize Your Music

The most obvious way to make money as a musician is by creating and selling your music. With digital distribution platforms like Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music, it's easier than ever to release your music to a global audience. You can also sell your music on platforms like Bandcamp and CD Baby. To maximize your earnings, make sure to register your songs with a performing rights organization like ASCAP or BMI, which will collect royalties for your performances and songwriting.

Get Paid to Perform

Performing live is another great way to make money as a musician. You can play at local bars and clubs, weddings, corporate events, and festivals. To get started, create a press kit with your music, photos, and bio, and reach out to local venues and event planners. You can also use gig booking platforms like GigSalad and GigMasters to find paid gigs.

Teach Music Lessons

If you have expertise in a particular instrument or genre, you can make money by teaching music lessons. You can offer lessons in person or online through platforms like Skype or Zoom. To find students, you can advertise on social media, Craigslist, or local classifieds. You can also use online marketplaces like TakeLessons and LessonFace to find students and manage your lessons.

License Your Music

Another way to make money as a musician is by licensing your music for use in films, TV shows, commercials, and video games. You can sign up with a music licensing agency like AudioJungle, Artlist, or Musicbed, which will connect you with potential clients and handle the licensing process for you. Make sure to create instrumental versions of your songs, as they are often more versatile and in demand for licensing.

Sell Merchandise

If you have a strong fan base, you can make money by selling merchandise like t-shirts, posters, and vinyl records. You can set up an online store on platforms like Shopify or Big Cartel, or sell your merchandise at your shows. Make sure to design merchandise that reflects your brand and resonates with your audience.

Collaborate with Brands

Brands are always looking for creative ways to reach new audiences, and collaborating with musicians is a popular strategy. You can partner with brands to create sponsored content, perform at brand events, or endorse products. To find brand partnerships, reach out to brands that align with your values and target audience, or use influencer marketing platforms like Tribe and AspireIQ.

Join a Music Collective

A music collective is a group of musicians who work together to promote each other's music and collaborate on projects. By joining a collective, you can tap into a larger network of fans and industry connections, and gain exposure to new audiences. You can find collectives in your local music scene or online through social media and forums.

Stay Relevant and Engage Your Fans

Finally, to succeed as a musician in 2023, it's essential to stay relevant and engage with your fans. Keep up with the latest trends in music and social media, and use platforms like Instagram, TikTok, and YouTube to connect with your audience. Offer exclusive content and behind-the-scenes access to your music and life, and foster a community around your brand. By staying relevant and engaged, you can build a loyal fan base and increase your earning potential as a musician.

In Conclusion

While making a living as a musician can be challenging, there are many ways to monetize your music and build a sustainable career. By creating and selling your music, performing live, teaching music lessons, licensing your music, selling merchandise, collaborating with brands, joining a music collective, and engaging with your fans, you can increase your earning potential and achieve success as a musician in 2023.
