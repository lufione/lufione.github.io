How To Get Free Money Online In Kenya

Written by Oliver May 13, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Are you looking for ways to earn extra cash without leaving your home? If you're in Kenya, you're in luck! There are several ways to get free money online, and we'll show you how in this article.

1. Sign up for Online Survey Websites

There are several online survey websites that pay you for your opinion. All you have to do is sign up and fill out surveys. Some of the popular ones in Kenya are SurveySavvy, PaidViewpoint, and Panel Station.

Tip:

Make sure to fill out your profile completely, so you qualify for more surveys. Also, don't give out any sensitive information, such as your bank details.

2. Participate in Online Contests

Many companies and websites in Kenya hold online contests where you can win cash prizes. Look out for these contests and participate. Some of the popular ones are Safaricom's BLAZE BYOB and KCB Bank's Lion's Den.

Tip:

Read the terms and conditions carefully before participating. Some contests may require you to purchase a product or service.

3. Sell Your Skills Online

Do you have a skill, such as writing, graphic design, or programming? You can sell your services on freelancing websites, such as Upwork and Fiverr. Create a profile, showcase your skills, and start bidding on projects.

Tip:

Start with small projects to build your reputation and get good reviews. This will help you get more clients in the future.

4. Participate in Affiliate Marketing

Affiliate marketing is when you promote a company's products or services and earn a commission for every sale made through your unique link. You can sign up for affiliate programs, such as Jumia Affiliate and Kilimall Affiliate, and promote their products on your website or social media.

Tip:

Make sure to disclose that you're using affiliate links. Also, choose products that are relevant to your audience.

5. Use Cashback Apps

Cashback apps, such as JumiaPay and Pezesha, give you cashback for every purchase you make through their app. You can use this cashback to make other purchases or withdraw it to your bank account.

Tip:

Check the cashback rates before making a purchase. Also, look out for special promotions and deals.

6. Participate in Online Focus Groups

Similar to online surveys, online focus groups pay you for your opinion. However, unlike surveys, focus groups are more in-depth and require you to participate in a group discussion. Some of the popular ones in Kenya are Toluna and UserTesting.

Tip:

Make sure to have a stable internet connection and a quiet place to participate in the discussion.

7. Sell Your Stuff Online

Do you have items you no longer need, such as clothes, electronics, or furniture? You can sell them online on websites, such as Jumia and OLX. Take clear pictures, write a detailed description, and set a reasonable price.

Tip:

Respond to inquiries promptly and meet up in a safe public place to exchange the items.

8. Participate in Online Betting

If you're good at predicting sports outcomes, you can participate in online betting. Some of the popular ones in Kenya are SportPesa and Betin. However, be aware that betting can be addictive and risky.

Tip:

Set a budget and stick to it. Also, don't chase your losses.

9. Rent Out Your Property Online

Do you have a spare room or a vacation home? You can rent it out online on websites, such as Airbnb and Booking.com. Create a listing, set a price, and communicate with potential guests.

Tip:

Take clear pictures, write a detailed description, and respond to inquiries promptly. Also, make sure to read the terms and conditions carefully.

10. Participate in Online Trading

If you're knowledgeable about the stock market, you can participate in online trading. Some of the popular ones in Kenya are EGM Securities and Faida Investment Bank. However, be aware that trading can be risky and requires a lot of research.

Tip:

Start with a small amount and diversify your portfolio. Also, don't invest more than you can afford to lose.

With these tips, you can start earning free money online in Kenya. However, remember that it takes time, effort, and patience. Good luck!
