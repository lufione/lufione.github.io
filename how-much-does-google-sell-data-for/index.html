How Much Does Google Sell Data For

Written by Petter Jul 14, 2023 · 6 min read

Google is one of the biggest tech companies in the world, and they make a lot of money from advertising. But how much of that money comes from selling user data? In this article, we’ll explore the topic in depth and answer some common questions about how Google uses and sells data.

What is User Data?

User data refers to any information that is collected from users when they use a website or app. This can include things like their search history, location data, and even personal information like their name and email address. Companies like Google collect this data to improve their services and target advertising more effectively.

How Does Google Use User Data?

Google uses user data in a variety of ways. One of the main uses is to improve their search results. By analyzing user behavior and search history, Google can tailor search results to better match the user’s needs. They also use this data to target advertising more effectively, showing users ads that are more relevant to their interests.

Does Google Sell User Data?

Technically, Google doesn’t sell user data. Instead, they use the data to improve their services and target advertising. However, they do allow advertisers to target users based on their data, which effectively means that they are selling access to that data. Advertisers can choose to target users based on their search history, location data, and other factors.

How Much Money Does Google Make from Selling Data?

It’s difficult to say exactly how much money Google makes from selling data, as they don’t release this information publicly. However, analysts estimate that Google’s advertising revenue was around $147 billion in 2022. While not all of this money comes directly from selling user data, it’s clear that data plays a significant role in Google’s advertising business.

Is It Legal for Google to Use and Sell User Data?

Yes, it is legal for Google to use and sell user data. However, they are required to be transparent about their data collection practices and give users the option to opt out of certain types of data collection. Additionally, they are required to follow data privacy laws, such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in Europe.

How Can I Protect My Data When Using Google?

There are a few steps you can take to protect your data when using Google. First, make sure to read and understand Google’s privacy policy, and adjust your privacy settings as needed. You can also use privacy-focused search engines like DuckDuckGo instead of Google. Finally, consider using a virtual private network (VPN) to encrypt your internet traffic and protect your privacy.

Conclusion

While Google doesn’t technically sell user data, they do use it to improve their services and target advertising. It’s difficult to say exactly how much money they make from this, but it’s clear that data plays a significant role in their advertising business. By understanding how Google uses and collects data, you can take steps to protect your privacy and ensure that your data is used responsibly.
