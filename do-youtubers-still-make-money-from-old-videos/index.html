Do Youtubers Still Make Money From Old Videos

Written by Luffy May 05, 2023 Â· 6 min read

YouTube has been around for over a decade and has been a platform where people can share their content with the world, and even make money while doing so. A lot has changed since the early days of YouTube, and one of the questions that people often ask is whether YouTubers still make money from their old videos. In this article, we'll explore this question in detail.

How Do YouTubers Make Money?

Before we delve into whether YouTubers still make money from old videos, let's first understand how they make money in the first place. There are several ways YouTubers can earn money:

Ad Revenue

One of the most common ways YouTubers make money is through ad revenue. YouTube has a program called Google AdSense, which allows YouTubers to monetize their videos. When someone watches a video and an ad is shown, the YouTuber earns a portion of the ad revenue.

Sponsorships

YouTubers with a large following often get approached by brands for sponsorships. These sponsorships can be in the form of product placements, mentions, or even dedicated videos.

Merchandise Sales

Many YouTubers have turned to selling merchandise to their fans. This can include anything from t-shirts to phone cases to mugs.

Do YouTubers Still Make Money from Old Videos?

Now, back to the main question - do YouTubers still make money from old videos? The short answer is yes, they do.

YouTube videos are like digital real estate - they can continue to earn money long after they've been uploaded. As long as the videos are still being watched, the YouTuber can continue to earn ad revenue from them.

However, it's important to note that the amount of money earned from old videos may not be as much as newer videos. This is because ad revenue is calculated based on several factors, including the number of views, the type of ad, and the viewer's location.

How Can YouTubers Maximize Earnings from Old Videos?

If YouTubers want to maximize their earnings from old videos, there are a few things they can do:

Optimize for Search

By optimizing their videos for search engines, YouTubers can ensure that their videos continue to be found and watched long after they've been uploaded. This can include using relevant keywords in the title, description, and tags.

Promote on Social Media

YouTubers can also promote their older videos on social media platforms like Twitter, Facebook, and Instagram. This can help to drive more views and potentially more ad revenue.

Create a Playlist

Creating a playlist of similar videos can also help to increase views on older videos. This is because when someone watches one video in a playlist, YouTube will automatically play the next video in the playlist.

Conclusion

So, to answer the question - do YouTubers still make money from old videos? The answer is a resounding yes. While the amount of money earned may not be as much as newer videos, there are still ways to maximize earnings from older content. By optimizing for search, promoting on social media, and creating playlists, YouTubers can continue to earn money from their digital real estate for years to come.
