How To Make Money For 10 Year Olds In The Winter

Written by Christine Aug 26, 2023 · 7 min read

Winter is a great time for 10-year-olds to make money. With the holiday season around the corner, there are plenty of opportunities to earn some extra cash. Whether you want to save up for a new toy or treat your family to a special gift, here are some ideas to get you started.

Sell Hot Drinks and Snacks

During the winter, people love to warm up with a hot drink or snack. You can set up a small stand outside your house or at a local park and sell hot chocolate, coffee, tea, or hot cider. Don’t forget to offer some tasty snacks like cookies, muffins, or popcorn to go along with the drinks.

Shovel Snow

When it snows, people need help shoveling their driveways and sidewalks. Offer your services to your neighbors or put up flyers around your neighborhood. Charge a reasonable fee for your hard work, and don’t forget to dress warmly and stay safe while shoveling.

Wrap Gifts

With the holiday season in full swing, many people need help wrapping gifts. Offer your services to your family, friends, or neighbors, and charge a small fee for each gift you wrap. You can also create a festive wrapping station with colorful paper, ribbons, and bows to make your service more attractive.

Clean Cars

Winter can be tough on cars, and many people need help cleaning off snow, ice, and salt from their vehicles. Offer your services to your neighbors or put up flyers around your neighborhood. Charge a reasonable fee for your hard work, and don’t forget to dress warmly and bring all the necessary cleaning supplies.

Sell Crafts and Gifts

If you’re crafty, you can create some winter-themed crafts or gifts to sell. You can make snow globes, ornaments, scarves, hats, or mittens. You can sell them online, at local craft fairs, or at school events. Make sure you price your items competitively and showcase your creativity.

Offer Pet Services

Winter can be a busy time for pet owners who need help walking their dogs or taking care of their pets while they’re away. Offer your services to your neighbors or put up flyers around your neighborhood. Charge a reasonable fee for your hard work, and don’t forget to dress warmly and bring some treats for the pets.

Organize a Winter Fundraiser

If you want to make money for a good cause, you can organize a winter fundraiser. You can sell cookies, hot chocolate, or other treats, or you can organize a winter-themed event like a snowman-building contest or a winter carnival. Donate a portion of your earnings to a charity of your choice.

Offer Tutoring Services

If you’re good at a particular subject, you can offer tutoring services to your classmates or younger students. Charge a reasonable fee for your hard work, and make sure you have all the necessary materials and resources to help your students succeed.

Sell Winter Sports Equipment

If you have some extra winter sports equipment like skates, sleds, or snowboards, you can sell them online or at a local sports store. Make sure your equipment is in good condition, and price it competitively to attract buyers.

Offer Photography Services

Winter can be a beautiful season, and many people want to capture its magic with some photos. If you have a good camera and some photography skills, you can offer your services to your family, friends, or neighbors. Charge a reasonable fee for your hard work, and make sure you showcase your best shots.

Conclusion

These are just some of the ways 10-year-olds can make money in the winter. Remember to stay safe, dress warmly, and have fun while earning some extra cash. Who knows, you might even discover a new passion or talent along the way!
