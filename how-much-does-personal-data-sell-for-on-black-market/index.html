How Much Does Personal Data Sell For on Black Market

Personal data has become a valuable commodity on the black market. With the increasing number of data breaches and cyber attacks, it's essential to understand how much your personal data is worth on the black market. In this article, we'll discuss the current market value of personal data and how you can protect yourself from cybercriminals.

What is personal data?

Personal data is any information that can identify an individual, such as name, address, phone number, Social Security number, credit card information, and more. This information is often collected by companies, websites, and social media platforms to provide personalized services and advertising.

How is personal data sold on the black market?

Personal data is often sold on the dark web, a part of the internet that is not accessible through regular search engines. Cybercriminals use various methods to obtain personal data, such as phishing scams, malware, and hacking into databases. They then sell the information to the highest bidder on the dark web.

How much is personal data worth on the black market?

The value of personal data varies depending on the type of information, the number of records available, and the demand for the data. For example, credit card information can sell for as little as $5 per record, while medical records can sell for up to $1,000 per record. Social Security numbers and other identifying information can sell for $10-$30 per record.

What can you do to protect your personal data?

Protecting your personal data is essential to prevent identity theft and financial fraud. Here are some tips to keep your personal data safe:

  • Use strong and unique passwords for all your online accounts.
  • Enable two-factor authentication on all your accounts that offer it.
  • Avoid clicking on suspicious links or downloading attachments from unknown sources.
  • Regularly monitor your bank and credit card statements for any unauthorized activity.
  • Use a reputable antivirus and anti-malware software on your devices.

Conclusion

Personal data is a valuable commodity on the black market, and cybercriminals will continue to target individuals and companies to obtain this information. Understanding how much your personal data is worth and how it can be used by cybercriminals is the first step in protecting yourself from identity theft and financial fraud. By following the tips above, you can safeguard your personal data and prevent it from falling into the wrong hands.
