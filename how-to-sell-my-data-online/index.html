How To Sell Your Data Online A Step By Step Guide

Written by Petter Oct 03, 2023 · 7 min read

With the rise of big data, selling personal data has become a lucrative business. If you're interested in making some extra cash, selling your data could be an option. Here's how to do it:

Step 1: Understand What Data You Can Sell

Before you start selling your data, you need to know what data you have that is sellable. This includes your browsing history, social media activity, and even your location data. Make sure you're comfortable with selling this information before you proceed.

Step 2: Find a Data Broker

Data brokers act as middlemen between companies and individuals who want to sell their data. Do some research to find a reputable data broker that you're comfortable working with. Make sure to read reviews and check their privacy policies.

Step 3: Sign Up and Create an Account

Once you've found a data broker you want to work with, sign up and create an account. You'll be asked to provide some personal information, such as your name and address, so the broker can verify your identity.

Step 4: Connect Your Data Sources

After you've created an account, you'll need to connect your data sources. This could include your social media accounts, email accounts, and even your smartphone's GPS data.

Step 5: Choose Which Data to Sell

Once you've connected your data sources, you'll need to choose which data you want to sell. Most data brokers will give you the option to select which data you want to share and which data you want to keep private.

Step 6: Set Your Price

After you've chosen which data you want to sell, you'll need to set your price. Data brokers will typically offer a suggested price, but you're free to set your own price based on how valuable you think your data is.

Step 7: Wait for Offers

Once you've set your price, you'll need to wait for offers from companies who are interested in buying your data. This could take some time, so be patient.

Step 8: Accept an Offer

When you receive an offer, you'll need to review it carefully and decide if you want to accept it. Make sure to read the terms and conditions carefully and ensure that you're comfortable with the company and what they plan to do with your data.

Step 9: Get Paid

After you've accepted an offer, you'll need to provide the company with your payment information. Most data brokers will pay via PayPal or direct deposit.

Step 10: Monitor Your Data

Once you've sold your data, it's important to monitor your data sources to ensure that your data is being used appropriately. If you notice any suspicious activity, report it to your data broker immediately.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is selling my data legal?

A: Yes, in most cases selling your data is legal. However, it's important to read the terms and conditions carefully and ensure that you're comfortable with what the company plans to do with your data.

Q: How much can I make selling my data?

A: The amount you can make selling your data varies depending on the type of data you have and the demand for it. Some people make a few dollars a month, while others can make hundreds or even thousands of dollars a year.

Q: Will selling my data affect my credit score?

A: No, selling your data should not affect your credit score. However, it's important to monitor your data sources to ensure that your data is being used appropriately.

Q: Can I sell my data anonymously?

A: No, data brokers will require you to provide some personal information to verify your identity. However, most data brokers have strict privacy policies and will keep your personal information secure.

Tips and Tricks

  • Read the terms and conditions carefully before you sell your data
  • Choose a reputable data broker with a good privacy policy
  • Set your price based on the value of your data
  • Monitor your data sources to ensure that your data is being used appropriately
