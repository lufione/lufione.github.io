How Can You Make Money As A Child

Written by Oliver May 21, 2023 Â· 6 min read

As a child, you may not be able to work a traditional job, but there are still plenty of ways to make money. Whether you want to save up for a new toy or contribute to your college fund, here are some tips for earning money as a kid.

Babysitting

If you're responsible and enjoy spending time with younger children, babysitting can be a great way to make money. Ask your parents, family friends, or neighbors if they need a babysitter. Make sure you're comfortable with the children and have a plan for emergency situations.

Pet Sitting or Dog Walking

If you love animals, pet sitting or dog walking can be a fun way to make money. Offer your services to neighbors or family friends who have pets. Make sure you're comfortable with the animals and have a plan for any unexpected situations.

Sell Crafts or Baked Goods

If you're creative, consider making and selling crafts or baked goods. You can sell your items at a local craft fair or set up an online store. Make sure you have your parents' permission and follow any local laws or regulations.

Yard Work

If you enjoy being outside, offer to do yard work for neighbors or family friends. This could include mowing lawns, raking leaves, or watering plants. Make sure you have the necessary equipment and are comfortable doing the work.

Tutoring

If you excel in a particular subject, offer to tutor younger children who need help. You can advertise your services at school or through online platforms. Make sure you're comfortable with the material and have a plan for each session.

Wash Cars

Offer to wash cars for neighbors or family friends. Make sure you have the necessary supplies and are comfortable doing the work. Consider offering additional services, such as waxing or detailing, for an extra fee.

Sell Items Online

If you have items you no longer need or want, consider selling them online. You can use platforms like eBay, Craigslist, or Facebook Marketplace. Make sure you have your parents' permission and understand the process for shipping or meeting buyers.

Collect and Recycle

Collect cans, bottles, or other recyclable items and take them to a recycling center. You can also offer to collect and recycle items for neighbors or family friends. Make sure you're familiar with the local recycling laws and have a plan for transporting the items.

Participate in Surveys or Focus Groups

Some companies offer surveys or focus groups for children to participate in. You can earn money or gift cards for sharing your opinions. Make sure you have your parents' permission and understand the rules and requirements for each opportunity.

Assist with Errands or Chores

Offer to assist neighbors or family friends with errands or chores. This could include grocery shopping, cleaning, or organizing. Make sure you're comfortable with the tasks and have a plan for completing them.

Final Thoughts

There are many ways for children to make money, but it's important to be safe and responsible. Always get your parents' permission before starting any money-making activities and make sure you understand any laws or regulations. With some effort and creativity, you can start earning money and reaching your financial goals.
