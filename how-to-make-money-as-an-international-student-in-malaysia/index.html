How To Make Money As An International Student In Malaysia

Written by William Oct 03, 2023 · 6 min read

Studying abroad can be an exciting and enriching experience, but it can also be expensive. As an international student in Malaysia, you may find that you need to supplement your income in order to make ends meet. Fortunately, there are many ways to make money while you study. Here are some tips and tricks to help you get started.

1. Part-Time Jobs

One of the most common ways for international students in Malaysia to make money is by working part-time. There are many opportunities available, such as working in a restaurant, retail store, or as a tutor. You can find job postings on websites like Indeed or JobStreet, or by asking around on campus.

It's important to note that international students in Malaysia are legally allowed to work up to 20 hours per week during the semester and full-time during semester breaks. Be sure to check with your institution for any specific regulations.

2. Freelancing

If you have skills in areas like writing, graphic design, or programming, you can also consider freelancing. Websites like Upwork or Fiverr allow you to find clients from all over the world and work on projects from the comfort of your own home. This can be a great way to earn extra income while still prioritizing your studies.

3. Sell Items Online

Another option is to sell items online. If you have items you no longer need or want to sell, you can use platforms like Shopee or Lazada to list them for sale. Alternatively, you can also consider dropshipping, which involves selling items from a third-party supplier without holding inventory.

4. Participate in Surveys or Focus Groups

Participating in surveys or focus groups can also be a way to earn extra income. Companies are always looking for feedback on their products or services, and they are often willing to pay for it. Websites like Toluna or Swagbucks offer opportunities to participate in surveys or focus groups in exchange for cash or gift cards.

5. Start a Blog or YouTube Channel

If you have a passion for writing or creating content, starting a blog or YouTube channel can be a way to monetize your interests. You can earn money through sponsorships, advertising, or by selling products related to your niche. While it may take some time to build an audience, it can be a rewarding way to earn extra income.

6. Rent Out Your Space

If you have extra space in your dorm or apartment, you can also consider renting it out. Platforms like Airbnb allow you to list your space for short-term rentals. However, be sure to check with your institution for any regulations or restrictions on renting out your space.

7. Translation Services

Finally, if you are fluent in multiple languages, you can also consider offering translation services. Many companies need documents or websites translated, and they are often willing to pay for it. Websites like TranslatorsCafé or ProZ can connect you with clients in need of translation services.

Conclusion

As an international student in Malaysia, there are many ways to make money while you study. Whether it's through part-time jobs, freelancing, or selling items online, there are plenty of opportunities to earn extra income. By exploring these options, you can make the most of your time abroad and ease any financial burdens you may have.
