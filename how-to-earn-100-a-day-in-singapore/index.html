How To Earn 100 A Day In Singapore

Written by Eveline Apr 27, 2023 · 6 min read

Living in Singapore can be expensive, but there are ways to earn some extra cash on the side. Whether you are a student, a stay-at-home parent, or someone looking for a side hustle, there are plenty of opportunities to earn $100 a day or more.

Freelancing

If you have a skill that can be done remotely, freelancing is a great way to earn money. Websites like Fiverr, Upwork, and Freelancer.com allow you to create a profile and offer your services to clients around the world. Some popular categories include graphic design, writing, programming, and social media management.

Ridesharing

If you have a car, you can sign up to be a rideshare driver with companies like Grab or Gojek. These companies allow you to set your own schedule and earn money by driving passengers around the city. You can earn even more by working during peak hours or driving for multiple companies.

Selling Online

If you have items you no longer need, you can sell them online through platforms like Carousell or eBay. You can also create your own online store on platforms like Shopify or Lazada. Popular items to sell include clothes, electronics, and handmade crafts.

Tutoring

If you excel in a particular subject, you can offer tutoring services to students. You can advertise your services on social media or tutor matching websites like SmileTutor or Superprof. Rates can range from $20 to $50 per hour depending on the subject and level of expertise.

Part-Time Jobs

There are plenty of part-time jobs available in Singapore, especially in the food and retail industries. You can apply for positions through job portals like Indeed or JobStreet. It’s important to note that some part-time jobs may require a work permit for foreigners.

Paid Surveys

You can earn money by participating in paid surveys online. Websites like Toluna, Swagbucks, and OpinionWorld will pay you for your opinions on various topics. The amount you can earn per survey varies, but you can typically earn $1 to $5 per survey.

Mystery Shopping

You can get paid to shop by becoming a mystery shopper. Companies like Market Force and Bestmark hire mystery shoppers to evaluate the customer experience at various stores and restaurants. You can earn up to $20 per assignment depending on the type of evaluation required.

Pet Sitting

If you love animals, you can offer pet sitting services to pet owners. Websites like Pawshake and PetBacker allow you to create a profile and connect with pet owners in need of pet care services. Rates can range from $10 to $30 per hour depending on the type of service required.

Blogging

If you enjoy writing and have a niche topic you’re passionate about, you can start a blog and earn money through advertising, sponsorships, and affiliate marketing. You can use platforms like WordPress or Blogger to create and publish your content.

Conclusion

Earning $100 a day in Singapore is achievable with the right mindset and effort. Whether you choose to freelance, rideshare, sell online, tutor, work part-time, participate in paid surveys, become a mystery shopper, pet sit, or start a blog, there are plenty of opportunities available. It’s important to find something you enjoy and are good at, and to treat it like a business by setting goals and managing your time effectively. With determination and hard work, you can reach your financial goals.
