How Can I Make My Money Tree Grow Faster

Written by Oliver Sep 02, 2023 Â· 6 min read

Money trees, scientifically known as Pachira aquatica, are popular indoor plants that are believed to bring good luck and prosperity to those who own them. These plants are known to grow slowly, but with the right care and attention, you can help your money tree grow faster and healthier. In this article, we will share some tips and tricks on how to make your money tree grow faster.

Choose the Right Pot

The first step in making your money tree grow faster is to choose the right pot. The pot you choose should be large enough to accommodate the plant's growing roots. It should also have drainage holes to prevent water from getting stagnant at the bottom.

Use the Right Soil

Money trees prefer moist, well-draining soil that is rich in nutrients. You can use a mix of potting soil, peat moss, and perlite to create the perfect soil mix for your money tree. Make sure to mix the soil well and add some fertilizer to provide the plant with the necessary nutrients for growth.

Water Regularly

Money trees require regular watering to keep them healthy and growing. You should water your money tree once a week or when the top inch of soil feels dry to the touch. Make sure to water the plant thoroughly and allow the excess water to drain out of the pot.

Provide Adequate Light

Money trees thrive in bright, indirect light. You should place your money tree near a window that receives plenty of sunlight but avoid direct sunlight as it can scorch the plant's leaves. If your money tree is not getting enough light, you can use artificial grow lights to supplement its light needs.

Prune Regularly

Pruning is an essential part of money tree care as it helps to promote growth and prevent the plant from becoming too leggy. You should prune your money tree regularly, especially during the growing season, to remove dead or damaged leaves and encourage new growth.

Use Fertilizer

Money trees require regular fertilization to provide them with the necessary nutrients for growth. You can use a balanced liquid fertilizer or slow-release fertilizer pellets to feed your money tree. Make sure to follow the instructions on the fertilizer package to avoid over-fertilizing the plant.

Provide Humidity

Money trees prefer a humid environment. You can increase the humidity around your plant by placing a tray of water near it or using a humidifier. You can also mist the plant's leaves regularly to provide it with the necessary moisture.

Repot When Necessary

Money trees can outgrow their pots quickly, and you may need to repot them every few years to provide them with enough space to grow. You should repot your money tree into a slightly larger pot using fresh soil and follow the same care routine as before.

Keep an Eye on Pests

Money trees are susceptible to pests such as mealybugs and spider mites. You should regularly inspect your plant for signs of pests and take necessary measures to eliminate them. You can use insecticidal soap or neem oil to get rid of pests.

Conclusion

By following these tips and tricks, you can help your money tree grow faster and healthier. Remember to provide your plant with adequate light, water, and nutrients, prune regularly, and keep an eye on pests. With proper care and attention, your money tree will bring you good luck and prosperity for years to come.
