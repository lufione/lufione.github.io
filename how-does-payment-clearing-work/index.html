How Does Payment Clearing Work

Written by Petter Jun 25, 2023 · 5 min read

Payment clearing is an essential process that takes place when a customer makes a payment to a merchant or seller. It involves multiple parties, including the customer's bank, the merchant's bank, and payment processors. In this article, we will explain how payment clearing works and what happens behind the scenes.

What is Payment Clearing?

Payment clearing is the process of transferring money between two banks when a customer makes a payment to a merchant. The payment clearing process ensures that the funds are transferred securely and efficiently, and that the payment is properly authorized and authenticated.

Step-by-Step Guide to Payment Clearing

Step 1: Customer Makes a Payment

The payment clearing process begins when a customer makes a payment to a merchant. The customer may make the payment online, in-store, or through a mobile device.

Step 2: Payment Authorization

Once the payment is made, the customer's bank needs to authorize the payment. The bank checks to ensure that the customer has sufficient funds in their account to cover the payment. If the funds are available, the bank approves the payment and sends a message to the payment processor.

Step 3: Payment Processor

The payment processor is a third-party company that facilitates the payment transaction between the customer's bank and the merchant's bank. The payment processor receives the payment authorization message from the customer's bank and forwards it to the merchant's bank. The payment processor also deducts a fee for their services.

Step 4: Merchant Receives Payment

Once the payment processor receives the payment authorization message from the customer's bank, they forward the payment to the merchant's bank. The merchant's bank receives the payment and credits the merchant's account with the payment amount, minus any fees charged by the bank.

FAQs

What Happens if the Customer Does Not Have Enough Funds?

If the customer does not have sufficient funds in their account, the payment authorization will be declined, and the payment will not go through. The customer may also be charged a non-sufficient funds (NSF) fee by their bank.

How Long Does Payment Clearing Take?

The payment clearing process typically takes 2-3 business days to complete. However, the exact time may vary depending on the banks involved and the payment method used.

Are Payment Clearing Fees the Same for All Payment Methods?

No, payment clearing fees may vary depending on the payment method used. For example, credit card payments may have higher fees compared to debit card payments.

Tips and Tricks

Here are some tips and tricks to ensure a smooth payment clearing process:

  • Ensure that you have sufficient funds in your account before making a payment.
  • Choose a payment method with lower fees to save money.
  • Check your bank statements regularly to ensure that all payments are properly authorized and cleared.

Conclusion

Payment clearing is an essential process that enables secure and efficient payment transactions between customers and merchants. Understanding how payment clearing works can help you make informed decisions when making payments and ensure that your payments are properly authorized and authenticated.
