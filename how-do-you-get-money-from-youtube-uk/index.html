How Do You Get Money From Youtube Uk

Written by Robby Apr 21, 2023 Â· 7 min read

YouTube is the world's largest video-sharing platform, and it offers an incredible opportunity to make money online. If you are a content creator in the UK, you might be wondering how to monetize your YouTube channel and earn revenue. In this article, we will discuss the various ways to get money from YouTube UK.

1. YouTube Partner Program

The most common way to make money on YouTube is through the YouTube Partner Program (YPP). To join the YPP, you need to have at least 1000 subscribers and 4000 watch hours in the past 12 months. Once you are accepted into the program, you can monetize your videos with ads and earn a share of the revenue generated.

What are the requirements for the YPP?

As mentioned earlier, you need to have at least 1000 subscribers and 4000 watch hours in the past 12 months to be eligible for the YPP. Additionally, you need to comply with YouTube's community guidelines and terms of service. Your content should be original, and you should not have any copyright strikes or community guidelines strikes on your channel.

2. Sponsorships and Brand Deals

Sponsorships and brand deals are another way to make money on YouTube. If you have a significant following and engagement on your channel, brands might approach you to promote their products or services in your videos. You can also reach out to companies and offer your services as a brand ambassador or influencer.

How do you find sponsorships and brand deals?

You can find sponsorships and brand deals by networking with other content creators, joining influencer marketing platforms, or reaching out to brands directly. Make sure to disclose any sponsored content in your videos to comply with advertising regulations.

3. Merchandise Sales

If you have a loyal fan base, you can sell merchandise such as t-shirts, mugs, and stickers on your channel. YouTube offers a merchandise shelf feature that allows you to showcase your products below your videos. You can also link to your merch store in your video descriptions and channel banner.

How do you set up a merchandise store on YouTube?

You can set up a merchandise store on YouTube by joining the YouTube Partner Program and linking your channel to a merchandising platform such as Teespring or Crowdmade. Once you have created your products, you can showcase them on your channel and earn a share of the revenue generated.

4. Fan Funding

YouTube offers a fan funding feature that allows your viewers to donate money to your channel. This feature is available to content creators in the UK through the Super Chat and Super Stickers features. Super Chat allows viewers to highlight their messages during live streams, while Super Stickers are animated images that viewers can purchase to support your channel.

How do you enable fan funding on YouTube?

You can enable fan funding on YouTube by joining the YouTube Partner Program and enabling Super Chat and Super Stickers in your channel settings. You can also link to your Patreon or other crowdfunding platforms in your video descriptions and channel banner.

5. YouTube Premium Revenue

If you are a content creator in the UK, you can earn a share of the revenue generated by YouTube Premium subscribers who watch your videos. YouTube Premium is a paid subscription service that offers ad-free viewing, background play, and access to exclusive content.

How much money can you make from YouTube Premium?

The amount of money you can make from YouTube Premium depends on the number of subscribers who watch your videos and the revenue generated by the service. YouTube does not disclose the exact amount of money paid to content creators, but it is generally lower than the revenue generated by ads.

Conclusion

There are various ways to get money from YouTube UK, including the YouTube Partner Program, sponsorships and brand deals, merchandise sales, fan funding, and YouTube Premium revenue. To maximize your earnings, you should create high-quality content that resonates with your audience and promotes engagement. Remember to comply with YouTube's guidelines and regulations and disclose any sponsored content or fan funding in your videos.
