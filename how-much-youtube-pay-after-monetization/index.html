How Much Does Youtube Pay After Monetization

Written by Bardi Apr 27, 2023 · 6 min read

Introduction

YouTube has become a popular platform for creators to showcase their skills and reach out to millions of people worldwide. With increased popularity, YouTube has introduced monetization features that allow creators to earn money from their videos. However, many creators wonder how much they can earn from YouTube after monetization. In this article, we will explore the various factors that determine YouTube earnings and how much creators can expect to earn.

Factors Affecting YouTube Earnings

The amount of money a creator can earn from YouTube depends on several factors, such as the number of views, the type of ad, the location of viewers, and the advertiser's budget. YouTube's revenue-sharing model allows creators to earn 55% of the revenue generated from ads played before, during, or after their videos. The remaining 45% goes to YouTube.

Number of Views

The number of views a video receives is one of the significant factors in determining YouTube earnings. The more views a video gets, the higher the chances of earning more money. However, it is essential to note that not all views generate revenue. For example, views from countries where YouTube is not available or views from users who use ad-blockers do not generate revenue.

Type of Ad

The type of ad that appears on a video also plays a role in determining earnings. YouTube offers different types of ads, such as skippable ads, non-skippable ads, bumper ads, and display ads. Skippable ads generate revenue only when viewers watch them for at least 30 seconds or until the end. Non-skippable ads generate revenue but can be annoying to viewers. Bumper ads are short ads that cannot be skipped, and display ads appear beside the video player.

Location of Viewers

The location of viewers also affects YouTube earnings. Ads displayed to viewers in countries with high advertising costs, such as the United States, generate more revenue than ads displayed to viewers in countries with lower advertising costs, such as India and Brazil.

Advertiser's Budget

The advertiser's budget is another factor that can influence YouTube earnings. Advertisers bid on ad placement, and the highest bidder gets their ad displayed. Advertisers with a high budget can bid more and secure ad placement on popular videos, generating more revenue for creators.

How Much Can You Earn From YouTube?

The amount of money a creator can earn from YouTube varies and depends on the factors mentioned above. According to YouTube, creators can expect to earn between $0.01 to $0.03 per view from ads displayed on their videos. This means that a video with 100,000 views can earn between $1,000 to $3,000. However, earnings can vary significantly based on the factors mentioned above. For example, a video with a high number of views from viewers in the United States can generate more revenue than a video with the same number of views from viewers in India.

Conclusion

In conclusion, YouTube earnings depend on many factors, such as the number of views, the type of ad, the location of viewers, and the advertiser's budget. Creators can expect to earn between $0.01 to $0.03 per view from ads displayed on their videos. However, earnings can vary significantly, and it is essential to create engaging content and promote videos to increase views and revenue. With dedication and hard work, creators can earn a substantial income from YouTube monetization.
