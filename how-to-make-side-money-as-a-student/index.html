How To Make Side Money As A Student

Written by Petter Jun 19, 2023 Â· 7 min read

Being a student can be challenging, especially when it comes to finances. However, there are many ways to make some extra cash while still focusing on your studies. Here are some tips and tricks to help you make side money as a student.

Sell Your Stuff Online

If you have old clothes, textbooks, or other items lying around that you no longer need, consider selling them online. Websites like eBay, Amazon, and Facebook Marketplace are great places to start. Just make sure to take good pictures and write detailed descriptions to increase your chances of making a sale.

Become a Tutor

If you excel in a particular subject, consider becoming a tutor for other students. You can advertise your services on campus bulletin boards, social media, or tutoring websites like TutorMe or Chegg. Not only will you be helping others, but you'll also be earning some extra cash.

Participate in Online Surveys

Many companies are willing to pay for consumer opinions, and you can take advantage of this by participating in online surveys. Websites like Swagbucks, Survey Junkie, and Vindale Research offer rewards for completing surveys, such as gift cards or cash payments.

Freelance Your Skills

If you have a particular skill, such as graphic design or writing, consider freelancing your services. Websites like Fiverr, Upwork, and Freelancer allow you to create a profile and bid on jobs that match your skills. This can be a great way to build your portfolio and make some extra money.

Start a Blog or YouTube Channel

If you have a passion for writing or creating videos, consider starting a blog or YouTube channel. Once you have built up a following, you can monetize your content through advertising, sponsorships, and affiliate marketing. Just make sure to create high-quality content that resonates with your audience.

Participate in Focus Groups

Focus groups are a great way to provide feedback on new products and services while getting paid for your time. You can find focus group opportunities through market research companies like Nielsen, Ipsos, and Focus Pointe Global.

Babysit or Pet-Sit

If you enjoy working with children or animals, consider offering your services as a babysitter or pet-sitter. You can advertise your services on social media or through word of mouth, and you can set your own rates based on your experience and availability.

Deliver Food or Packages

If you have a car or bike, consider delivering food or packages for companies like Uber Eats, DoorDash, or Postmates. You can choose your own hours and earn money based on the number of deliveries you make.

Rent Out Your Space

If you have a spare room or couch, consider renting it out on websites like Airbnb or Couchsurfing. This can be a great way to meet new people while earning some extra cash.

Take on Odd Jobs

Finally, don't be afraid to take on odd jobs like yard work, cleaning, or moving. You can advertise your services on social media or through websites like TaskRabbit or Craigslist. Just make sure to set clear expectations with your clients and charge fair rates based on the job.

In conclusion, there are many ways to make side money as a student. Whether you choose to sell your stuff online, become a tutor, participate in online surveys, freelance your skills, start a blog or YouTube channel, participate in focus groups, babysit or pet-sit, deliver food or packages, rent out your space, or take on odd jobs, there is something for everyone. Just remember to prioritize your studies and only take on jobs that fit your schedule and abilities.
