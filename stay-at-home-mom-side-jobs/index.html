Stay At Home Mom Side Jobs How To Earn Extra Income In 2023

Written by Daniel Apr 26, 2023 Â· 8 min read

Being a stay-at-home mom is a full-time job, but it doesn't have to mean sacrificing your financial goals. With the rise of digital technology and the gig economy, there are more opportunities than ever to earn extra income from home. Whether you're looking to pay off debt, save for a family vacation, or build up your emergency fund, here are some side jobs you can do while still being present for your children.

1. Virtual Assistant

If you're organized, detail-oriented, and tech-savvy, you can offer your services as a virtual assistant. This job involves handling administrative tasks for busy professionals, such as scheduling appointments, managing email accounts, and updating social media profiles. You can find virtual assistant jobs on freelance marketplaces like Upwork and Fiverr, or you can start your own business and market your services to your own network.

2. Online Tutor

If you have expertise in a particular subject, you can offer online tutoring services. This job typically involves working with students one-on-one or in small groups, using video conferencing software like Zoom or Skype. You can find online tutoring jobs on websites like Chegg and TutorMe, or you can create your own website and advertise your services on social media.

3. Freelance Writer

If you have a way with words, you can offer your writing services to businesses and publications. This job can involve writing blog posts, articles, marketing materials, and more. As a freelance writer, you can set your own rates and work on projects that interest you. You can find freelance writing jobs on websites like Contena and ProBlogger, or you can pitch your own ideas to publications and websites.

4. Etsy Seller

If you're crafty, you can open an Etsy shop and sell your handmade goods. This job can involve making jewelry, clothing, home decor items, and more. As an Etsy seller, you can set your own prices and work on your own schedule. You can also participate in craft fairs and markets to expand your customer base.

5. Social Media Manager

If you're a social media whiz, you can offer your services as a social media manager. This job involves creating and scheduling posts, engaging with followers, and analyzing analytics to improve performance. You can find social media manager jobs on freelance marketplaces like Freelancer and Indeed, or you can start your own social media management business and market your services to businesses in your area.

6. Graphic Designer

If you have design skills, you can offer your services as a graphic designer. This job can involve creating logos, website designs, marketing materials, and more. You can find graphic design jobs on websites like 99designs and DesignCrowd, or you can start your own business and market your services to your own network.

7. Online Reseller

If you're good at finding bargains, you can offer your services as an online reseller. This job involves buying items at a low price and reselling them at a higher price on platforms like eBay and Amazon. You can find items to resell at garage sales, thrift stores, and online marketplaces like Facebook Marketplace and Letgo.

8. Pet Sitter

If you're an animal lover, you can offer your services as a pet sitter. This job involves taking care of pets while their owners are away, including feeding, walking, and playing with them. You can find pet sitting jobs on websites like Rover and Care.com, or you can advertise your services to your own network.

9. Online Survey Taker

If you have free time and want to earn some extra cash, you can take online surveys. This job involves giving your opinions on products and services in exchange for payment. You can find online survey jobs on websites like Swagbucks and Survey Junkie.

10. Personal Shopper

If you love fashion, you can offer your services as a personal shopper. This job involves helping clients choose outfits and accessories, either in person or online. You can find personal shopping jobs on websites like Stitch Fix and Trunk Club, or you can start your own personal shopping business and market your services to your own network.

Conclusion

As a stay-at-home mom, you have a unique opportunity to earn extra income without sacrificing your family time. With a little creativity and effort, you can find a side job that works for you and your family's needs. Whether you're looking to pursue a passion or just make some extra cash, these side jobs can help you achieve your financial goals in 2023.
